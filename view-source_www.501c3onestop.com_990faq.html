990s FAQ

  1. Home
  2. FAQ

Our 501c3 tax-exempt organization didn’t have any activity last year, do we still have to file a nonprofit tax return?

Yes, you do. Your nonprofit might have had “zero” financials and it is still required to file a nonprofit tax return in order to stay in compliance with the government.

What will happen if we miss filing our nonprofit’s tax returns for 3 years or more?

If you skip on filing your nonprofit’s tax returns for 3 years, the IRS are going to automatically revoke your 501c3 tax-exempt status. This means your charity will continue operating as a nonprofit organization, however the donations, grants and contributions won’t be tax deductible. The only way to get your 501c3 tax-exempt status reinstated is to file your 501c3 application again and pay the filing fee.

Can we extend the period for filing our nonprofit’s tax return?

You can file an automatic 6 months extension in order to prolong the period for filing. The new date for filing your nonprofit's tax return after the extension is November 15th.

How can we file f990-N/e- postcard?

Form 990-N can be e-filed only. You do it directly on the IRS website or use a tax software. A log in and password will be needed for first time filers.

We plan on registering with GuideStar. What nonprofit tax return to file?

As GuideStar provides lots of opportunities for nonprofits, you’d better file form 990-EZ or form 990.

Are we going to owe taxes to the IRS with our form 990s?

Nonprofit tax returns are informational returns. This means your 501c3 organization won’t owe taxes unless you had an unrelated business activity (UBI) during the year. The UBI is reported on a separate tax form 990-T, which is different from your nonprofit’s tax return.

Does the state require a copy of our 990s form?

Some states, such as Georgia, will require a copy of your nonprofit’s tax return when you renew your solicitation registration. Other states will accept the audited financial statements only.

Who can sign our nonprofit’s tax return f990s?

Only a board member can sign your nonprofit tax return. Nonprofit tax returns – form 990-N (electronically signed), form 990-EZ or form 990 can be signed by any board member serving on the board for the reported year. A signature of one authorized board member is accepted.

Which board members to include in our nonprofit’s form 990?

You need to list all board members that served on the board for the reported year. This includes current and former board members.

Do we need to file additional schedules with form 990-EZ and form 990?

Additional schedules are required based on the nonprofit’s activities. All 501c3 organizations filing form 990-EZ or form 990 are required to file Schedule A; if the nonprofit had large donations of greater than $5,000 for the year, Schedule B is required; if the 501c3 organization is a school, Schedule E is required; etc.
